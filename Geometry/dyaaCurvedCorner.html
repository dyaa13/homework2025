Core Ideas

The “curved corner” problems usually come from subtracting a quarter-circle from a square. When the radius equals the side length s, the quarter-circle area is πs²/4.

Common result: Shaded (square minus quarter-circle)
= s² − πs²/4 = s²(1 − π/4)   (use π = 3.14)
Pattern Area Formula Notes
Square − quarter circle s²(1 − π/4) Radius = side length
Two identical curved corners 2·s²(1 − π/4) Often appears in a 2×2 big square
Square − 90° circular segment s² − s²(π − 2)/4 Segment = (sector − right triangle)
Tip: When you see a curved boundary inside a square, ask: “Is it a quarter circle?” If yes, find its radius and subtract from the square.
