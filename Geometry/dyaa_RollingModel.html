Key Idea

A circle of radius r rolls once around the outside of a polygon and returns to the start. The circle “sweeps” a band around the shape.

Swept area depends only on the polygon perimeter P:

Swept Area = (Perimeter × Diameter) + Area of the circle
A = P × (2r) + πr² (use π = 3.14)
Shape Perimeter P Swept Area
Square (side s) 4s 4s·(2r) + πr²
Rectangle (L × W) 2(L + W) 2(L+W)·(2r) + πr²
Any convex polygon sum of side lengths P·(2r) + πr²
Remember: you do not need the angles. Just find the perimeter, then use A = P·(2r) + πr².
