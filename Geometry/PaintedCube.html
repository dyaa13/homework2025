How Many Small Cubes Have 0 / 1 / 2 / 3 Painted Faces?

Paint the entire surface of a rectangular prism, then cut it into n × m × k small cubes (unit cubes or larger equal cubes). The number of painted faces on a small cube depends on whether it is at a corner, on an edge, on a face, or inside the solid.

Painted faces Where the small cube sits Count (for n,m,k ≥ 2)
3 faces Corner cubes 8
2 faces Edge cubes (excluding corners) 4[(n−2)+(m−2)+(k−2)]
1 face Face-interior cubes (excluding edges) 2[(n−2)(m−2)+(n−2)(k−2)+(m−2)(k−2)]
0 faces Inside cubes (n−2)(m−2)(k−2)
Tip: If the big solid is L × W × H cm and each small cube is s cm on an edge, then the number of small cubes along each direction is: n = L/s, m = W/s, k = H/s (so L, W, H must be multiples of s).

In the diagrams, the painted outer faces are shaded red, and the grid shows how it is split into smaller equal cubes.
