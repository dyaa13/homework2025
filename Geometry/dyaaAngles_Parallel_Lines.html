Core Ideas

When a transversal cuts two parallel lines, special angle relationships appear. Your job is to identify the angle relationship, then calculate the missing angle (or solve for x).

Skill Simple method Reminder
Corresponding Same “corner position” → equal Works only if the lines are parallel.
Alternate interior Inside the lines, opposite sides of transversal → equal Look for the angles “in between” the two lines.
Co-interior Inside the lines, same side of transversal → sum = 180° Often a 2-step question with straight-line angles.
Quick example
If two angles are co-interior and one is 112°, then the other is 180° − 112° = 68°.
Tip: In harder questions, you may need 2 steps, e.g. vertical opposite first, then use corresponding / alternate / co-interior.
