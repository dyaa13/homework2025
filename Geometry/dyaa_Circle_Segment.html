Core Ideas

A circular segment (an “arch” shape) is often found by: Segment Area = Sector Area − Triangle Area.

For a 90° sector with radius r:
Sector Area = πr² / 4
Right Triangle Area = r² / 2
Segment Area = r²(π − 2) / 4  (use π = 3.14)
Pattern Shaded Area Formula Notes
1 quarter-circle segment r²(π − 2)/4 90° sector minus right triangle
Lens in a square (two quarter circles) s²(π − 2)/2 Two identical 90° segments
One “petal” in the 4-petal square s²(π − 2)/8 Side length is s; each circle radius is s/2
All 4 petals together s²(π − 2)/2 4 × (one petal)
Tip: If you can break the shaded shape into repeated identical segments, compute one and multiply.
