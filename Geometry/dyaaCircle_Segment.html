Key idea

  • Circular segment area = sector area − triangle area (triangle is made by the two radii and the chord).
  • Sector area: (θ/360) × π × r² where θ is the central angle (degrees).
  • Triangle area (two sides r and included angle θ): ½ × r² × sin(θ).
  • For a 90° segment: ¼ × π × r² − ½ × r² = r²(π − 2)/4.
  • Many “arched / petal / lens” shapes are built from multiple identical 90° segments plus simple shapes (square/rectangle/circle).

Worked example

A 90° segment has radius 10 cm. Find the shaded area.

Area = ¼ × 3.14 × 10² − ½ × 10²
= 78.50 − 50.00 = 28.50 cm²
