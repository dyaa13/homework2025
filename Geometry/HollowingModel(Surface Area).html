Key Idea: Surface Area Change After Cutting a “Hole”

When you cut out a small cube/cuboid from a solid, some outside faces disappear, but new inside faces appear (the walls and “floor” of the hole).

A reliable method is: New Surface Area = Original Surface Area − (area removed from outside) + (new inside area exposed).

Shortcut (for a cut-out cuboid of size a × b × c):

Surface area of the removed cuboid is 2(ab + ac + bc). If the removed cuboid is flush with some outer faces of the big solid, those flush faces were “outside” and will be removed. The other faces become inside walls.

Classic cube notch (small cube side = t):
• Cut at a corner (touches 3 faces) → surface area change = 0
• Cut on an edge (touches 2 faces) → surface area change = +2t²
• Cut on a face (touches 1 face) → surface area change = +4t²
(Because more inside walls are added than outside squares removed.)
