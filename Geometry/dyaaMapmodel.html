Core Ideas

These problems are about counting all rectangles or all squares in a grid. A grid with m rows and n columns has (m+1) horizontal grid lines and (n+1) vertical grid lines.

Type Easy method (student-friendly) What to do
Count all rectangles in an m×n grid 1) Count horizontal lines: m + 1
2) Count vertical lines: n + 1
3) Rectangles = (ways to pick 2 horizontal lines) × (ways to pick 2 vertical lines) 		Pick the top & bottom first, then pick the left & right.
Rectangles that contain ☆ Count choices:
• Top boundary choices = number of rows above or at ☆
• Bottom boundary choices = number of rows below or at ☆
• Left boundary choices = number of columns left of or at ☆
• Right boundary choices = number of columns right of or at ☆
Multiply all four numbers. 		Think: “How many ways can I make a rectangle around ☆?”
Count all squares in an m×n grid Count by size:
• 1×1 squares: m × n
• 2×2 squares: (m−1) × (n−1)
• 3×3 squares: (m−2) × (n−2)
Continue until you cannot fit the size. 		Add them up: 1×1 + 2×2 + 3×3 + …
Example: 3×3 grid (count squares)
1×1: 3×3 = 9
2×2: 2×2 = 4
3×3: 1×1 = 1
Total squares = 9 + 4 + 1 = 14
Tip: Write down m and n first. Then decide whether you are counting rectangles, ☆-rectangles, or squares.
Instructions: Enter an integer. (No units needed.)
