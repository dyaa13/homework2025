Key Idea

When equal circles are tightly packed and you wrap a rope around the outside, the boundary is made of straight tangents + circular arcs.

For equal circles of diameter d, the total arc part always adds up to a full turn: arc length = πd (because the rope turns 360° in total).
So: Rope length = (sum of outside straight parts) + πd, with π = 3.14.
