1) What is a Fraction?

A fraction shows equal parts of a whole (or of a group). The numerator is the top number and tells how many parts we have. The denominator is the bottom number and tells how many equal parts the whole is split into.

Example: 34 means ‚Äú3 parts out of 4 equal parts‚Äù.

Types: Proper (numerator < denominator), Improper (numerator ‚â• denominator), Mixed number (whole number + fraction).

Example mixed number: 2 13

2) Equivalent Fractions

Two fractions are equivalent if they represent the same value. You can make equivalent fractions by multiplying or dividing the numerator and denominator by the same non-zero number.

Example: 12 = 24 (√ó2 on top and bottom)

3) Simplifying (Reducing)

To simplify a fraction, divide the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (GCF) so the numbers are as small as possible.

Example: 1218 ‚Üí divide by 6 ‚Üí 23

Steps

  1. Find the GCF of numerator and denominator.
  2. Divide both by the GCF.
  3. Check there is no common factor greater than 1.

Tip: If the numerator is a factor of the denominator, the simplified result may be a whole number. Example: 63 = 2

  • Same numerator: smaller denominator ‚Üí bigger fraction.
