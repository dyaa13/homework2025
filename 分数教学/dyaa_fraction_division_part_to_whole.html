Fractions — Division: From Part to Whole

DYAA EDUCATION

Find the whole (part → total) • Remaining fractions • Mixed numbers

Student:

1) Key rule

To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.

a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c
Tip: Keep the first fraction, Change ÷ to ×, Flip the second fraction.

2) From part to whole (Part → Total)

If a fraction of the whole equals a known amount, you can find the whole by dividing by the fraction.

fraction × whole = part
whole = part ÷ fraction
Example: 3/5 of a tank is 12 L.
Whole = 12 ÷ 3/5 = 12 × 5/3 = 20 L.
If a question says “1/4 was used”, then the amount left is 1 − 1/4 = 3/4 of the whole.

3) Mixed numbers

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before dividing.

Example: 1 1/2 = 3/2
Then: 3/2 ÷ 1/4 = 3/2 × 4/1

4) Always simplify

After multiplying, simplify the result.

Example: (3/4) × (8/3) = 2

5) Input format (Practice)

You can type your answers as:

Fraction: 7/8
Mixed: 2 1/3
Integer: 5

6) Common mistake

Do not divide the numerators and denominators separately.

Correct method: multiply by the reciprocal.
