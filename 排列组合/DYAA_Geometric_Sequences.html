Geometric Sequences — Review

A geometric sequence (also called a geometric progression, GP) is a sequence where each term is made by multiplying by the same number each time. That number is the common ratio, written as r.

Key ideas

  • First term: a1
  • Common ratio: r = an ÷ an−1 (for consecutive terms)
  • nth term: an = a1 × rn−1
  • Sum of first n terms (r ≠ 1): Sn = a1 × (rn − 1) ÷ (r − 1)
Common workflow:
1) Identify a1 and r (or find them from given terms).
2) Use the nth-term formula to find a specific term.
3) Use the sum formula to find total of the first n terms.

Quick checks

QuestionWhat to use
Find a specific term (e.g., a8) Use an = a1 × rn−1
Find the total of the first n terms Use Sn = a1(rn−1)/(r−1)
Find r from consecutive terms r = ak+1 ÷ ak

Worked examples

Example 1 — nth term (direct)

The sequence starts at 3 and multiplies by 2 each time: 3, 6, 12, 24, ...
Find a8.

a1 = 3, r = 2
a8 = a1 × r8−1 = 3 × 27 = 3 × 128 = 384
Answer: a8 = 384

Example 2 — sum of first n terms

A toy factory makes 5 toys on day 1 and triples the number each day.
Find the total number of toys made in the first 6 days.

a1 = 5, r = 3, n = 6
S6 = 5 × (36 − 1) ÷ (3 − 1)
= 5 × (729 − 1) ÷ 2 = 5 × 728 ÷ 2 = 1820
Answer: 1820

Example 3 — find r and a1, then solve

In a geometric sequence, a2 = 6 and a4 = 54.
Find a1 and r, then find S5.

a2 = a1r = 6
a4 = a1r3 = 54
Divide: a4 ÷ a2 = r2 = 54 ÷ 6 = 9 ⇒ r = 3
Then a1 = 6 ÷ 3 = 2

S5 = 2 × (35 − 1) ÷ (3 − 1) = 2 × (243 − 1) ÷ 2 = 242
Answer: a1=2, r=3, S5=242
