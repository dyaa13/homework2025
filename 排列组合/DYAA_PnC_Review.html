Permutations & Combinations — Review

This page reviews permutations and combinations. The key question is always: Does order matter?

Idea When to use Method
Permutation (arrangement) Order matters (positions, 1st/2nd/3rd, passwords) n! or P(n,r)
Combination (selection) Order does NOT matter (choose a team, choose toppings) C(n,r) (also written as nCr)
Connection Permutations vs combinations for the same n and r P(n,r) = C(n,r) × r!
Multi-step counting Different stages (some ordered, some not) Break into steps and multiply
Worked examples

Example 1 (Permutation): 7 different medals are arranged in a row.
Number of arrangements = 7! = 5040.

Example 2 (Combination): Choose 3 toppings from 8 different toppings for a pizza.
Order does not matter, so use combinations:
C(8,3) = 56.

Example 3 (Mixed): From 9 students, choose a President, a Vice-President (different roles), and then choose 2 committee members.
President & Vice-President: 9 × 8. Committee members: C(7,2).
Total = 9 × 8 × C(7,2) = 9 × 8 × 21 = 1512.
