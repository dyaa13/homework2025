1) Prime and Composite

A prime number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself.
A composite number has more than two factors.

Prime: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, ‚Ä¶
Composite: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12, 15, 16, ‚Ä¶
Important: 1 is not prime and not composite.

2) Factor tree (prime factorization)

A factor tree breaks a number into factors until all the leaves are prime. The order of the tree can be different, but the final prime factors are the same.

Example: 72
72 = 8 √ó 9
8 = 2 √ó 4 = 2 √ó 2 √ó 2
9 = 3 √ó 3
So 72 = 2 √ó 2 √ó 2 √ó 3 √ó 3 = 2¬≥ √ó 3¬≤.

3) Using factor trees to find HCF and LCM

Step 1: Prime factorize each number (factor tree).
Step 2: Compare prime factors.

HCF: take common primes with the smallest powers.
LCM: take all primes with the largest powers.
